3D Food Printer

The goal: design, fabricate, and test a 3D food printer that is cost-efficient, user-friendly, and safe for a home cook to create customizable edible creations directly from a digital file
The approach: an interactive, chocolate-printing device for the young adult looking to get creative or to show off to friends

The result: a fully-functional model showcased to UT Faculty and Students
External:
- Wood
- PLA plastic


Internal:
- Arduino Mega 2560
- RepRap Arduino Mega Polulu Shield 1.4
- Nema 17 Stepper Motor
- 7V Power Supply


Configured on:
- Arduino IDE Software
- Marlin Firmware
- Repetier-Host
