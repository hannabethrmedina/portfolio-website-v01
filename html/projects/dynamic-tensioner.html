DYNAMIC TENSIONER FOR TOTAL KNEE REPLACEMENT
Rombal, Inc.
The goal: design a medical device to be inserted in the patient's knee during total knee replacement surgery in order to obtain data on the unique alignment and provide surgeons with accurate information in real-time to aid in the surgical procedure and overall, improve patient satisfaction.


The research: 1 in 5 total knee replacement surgeries result in patient dissatisfaction, a fact that can be attributed not to the lack of skill of surgeons but on the current surgical methods and a lack of quantitative information about each patient’s unique alignment.

My team and I worked with start-up company Rombal, Inc. to design, model, and verify a design for a device capable of producing force and recording data on the patient’s knee during surgery. A detailed list of instructions for manufacturing and use, and a mock graphical-user interface (GUI) for the device were included.
